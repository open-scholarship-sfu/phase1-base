6 Strategy, Programming, Policies & Processes

6.1 Vision & Mission

  • Vision: Embed open scholarship practices across SFU, aligning with institutional frameworks for open science and open scholarship.
  • Mission: Build a vibrant community of practice supporting researchers with tools, training, incentives, and culture change.
  • Principles: Transparency, accessibility, reuse, and equity — consistent with SFU’s Open Science principles.

6.2 Strategic Goals

  • Increase adoption of open access publishing, open data, open educational resources, and open methods.
  • Build capacity through training, mentorship, and community collaboration.
  • Integrate open scholarship throughout the research lifecycle.
  • Evaluate and communicate impact through open reporting mechanisms.

6.3 Programming

  • Training: Workshops on open data, reproducibility, and licensing.
  • Community of Practice: Monthly meetings and special interest groups.
  • Champion Program: Faculty and student advocates for open scholarship.
  • Resources: Online repository of toolkits and templates.
  • Consultations: Support for researchers implementing open practices.
  • Events: Hackathons, seminars, “Open Scholarship Week,” annual showcase.

6.4 Policies & Processes

  • Reference SFU’s Open Scholarship and Open Access Policies.
  • Define clear onboarding/offboarding for members and leaders.
  • Workflow for proposal submission, review, and approval of new initiatives.
  • Conflict resolution and escalation linked to the Code of Conduct.
  • Budget allocation and funding process transparency.
